# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

OSINTForge is committed to providing a welcoming and inclusive environment for all contributors and community members, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, political belief, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, socioeconomic status, or technology choices.

We are dedicated to providing a harassment-free experience for everyone, and we do not tolerate harassment of participants in any form.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Be Respectful** - Treat all members with respect and courtesy
- **Be Inclusive** - Welcome people from different backgrounds and experiences
- **Be Patient** - Give others time to express themselves
- **Be Constructive** - Offer helpful feedback and suggestions
- **Be Honest** - Communicate openly and truthfully
- **Be Professional** - Maintain a professional tone in all interactions
- **Listen Actively** - Value others' perspectives and opinions
- **Own Your Mistakes** - Acknowledge and learn from errors

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, bullying, or intimidation of any kind
- Discrimination based on protected characteristics
- Offensive comments related to gender, sexual orientation, disability, race, or religion
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Personal attacks or derogatory comments
- Publishing private information without consent
- Threats of violence or violent language
- Trolling or inflammatory comments
- Spamming or unsolicited promotion
- Any form of discrimination or exclusion

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All OSINTForge spaces (GitHub, discussions, issues, pull requests)
- Official community events (online meetings, workshops)
- Interactions related to representing the project
- Any conduct that could reasonably affect the community's wellbeing

This Code of Conduct applies both within project spaces and in public spaces where an individual is representing the project or its community.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for:

- Clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate corrective action in response to violations
- Removing, editing, or rejecting contributions that don't align with this Code of Conduct
- Communicating expectations and maintaining transparency

## Reporting Guidelines

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

### How to Report

1. **Email**: [admin@osintintelligence.xyz] with subject "[CODE OF CONDUCT] Report"
2. **GitHub Issue**: Only if it doesn't involve personal safety concerns

### What to Include

- Description of the incident
- Who was involved
- When it occurred
- Where it occurred (link to issue, PR, discussion, etc.)
- Your contact information (optional but helpful)
- Any supporting evidence (screenshots, links)

### What Happens Next

1. We will acknowledge receipt within 48 hours
2. We will investigate the report confidentially
3. We will take appropriate action within 7 days
4. We will follow up with you about the resolution
5. If requested, your identity will be kept confidential

## Enforcement Actions

Depending on severity, actions may include:

1. **Warning** - Formal notice of violation
2. **Temporary Restriction** - Temporary ban from participation (1-7 days)
3. **Suspension** - Extended ban from community (up to 1 year)
4. **Permanent Ban** - Removal from the project permanently
5. **Other Actions** - Reporting to relevant authorities if needed

## Appeal Process

If you believe you were incorrectly sanctioned:

1. Email your appeal to [maintainer-email@example.com]
2. Include why you believe the action was unjust
3. Provide any additional context or evidence
4. A separate reviewer will reconsider the action
5. You'll receive a response within 14 days

## Community Guidelines

### Contributing

- **Be Constructive** - Offer helpful feedback on proposals
- **Respect Decisions** - Accept community decisions made by maintainers
- **Give Credit** - Acknowledge others' ideas and contributions
- **Quality Over Quantity** - Contribute meaningfully, not just for stats

### Discussion

- **Stay On Topic** - Keep discussions focused on the project
- **Search First** - Check existing issues before asking questions
- **Ask Clearly** - Provide context and details in your questions
- **Cite Sources** - If referencing external information, provide links

### Code Review

- **Be Respectful** - Reviews are about code, not the person
- **Be Thorough** - Test and verify changes before approving
- **Explain Why** - When suggesting changes, explain your reasoning
- **Be Timely** - Review PRs within a reasonable timeframe

### Conflict Resolution

If you have a conflict with another member:

1. **Try Direct Communication** - Message them privately if appropriate
2. **Stay Calm** - Avoid emotional language
3. **Find Common Ground** - Focus on shared goals
4. **Involve Moderators** - If needed, ask for help from maintainers
5. **Accept Resolution** - Work toward a solution that respects both parties

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: What if I accidentally violate the Code of Conduct?
**A:** We believe in education and growth. Most first-time violations result in a private conversation to clarify expectations. If you understand and correct the behavior, no further action is typically taken.

### Q: Is the Code of Conduct enforced equally?
**A:** Yes. All community members, including maintainers, are held to the same standards. No one is above the Code of Conduct.

### Q: What if I disagree with the Code of Conduct?
**A:** You're welcome to raise concerns via email or open a respectful discussion. However, acceptance of the Code of Conduct is required for participation in the community.

### Q: How can I support the Code of Conduct?
**A:** by following it yourself, defending others who are targeted, and reporting violations.

### Q: What if the reporter is also violating the Code of Conduct?
**A:** Both parties will be held accountable. Reports aren't a free pass to behave badly; they're about protecting the community.

## Summary

We expect all community members to:

✅ Be respectful and professional  
✅ Treat others with kindness and empathy  
✅ Communicate clearly and constructively  
✅ Welcome diverse perspectives and backgrounds  
✅ Hold themselves accountable for their behavior  
✅ Report violations to maintain a safe community
